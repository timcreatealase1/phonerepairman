How Option A was generated

Loaded PR Branding Font - Blackout Midnight.ttf with opentype.js, flattened every Bezier curve to a 32-segment polygon, ran clipper-lib with a -55 em-unit inward offset on the outer contour of every glyph in the set A B D O P Q R 0 4 6 8 a b d e g o p q, reversed the resulting polygon's winding direction, and appended it as an inner ring so TrueType's non-zero fill rule cuts it as a hole. 19 glyphs modified.

  • Will look good: O · P · D · B · 0 · 8 · b · d · o · p · q — simple bowls.
  • Will look passable: A · Q — single counter with predictable shape.
  • Will look amateurish: R · 4 · 6 · a · e · g — counters are part of the letterform aesthetic, not just "shrink-outline-inward".

Output is a free derivative — Blackout Midnight is licensed for personal/commercial use; this is a transformation of a font you legally own. Filename: blackout-midnight-counters.ttf.

Option B note

Bebas Neue is on Google Fonts (free, open-source). It's a heavy condensed all-caps display face by Ryoichi Tsunekawa — the closest type-design relative to Blackout Midnight that ships with proper counters. If picked, the wordmark PNG stays in Blackout Midnight (your identity asset); only the live site headings switch to Bebas Neue. Loads via a Google Fonts <link> — zero file hosting on your side.
