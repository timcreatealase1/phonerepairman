# Phone Repairman — Photography brief

> Single-page brief for a local photographer. Print and bring on the day.

---

## The business in one paragraph

Phone Repairman is a device repair shop at **22 Bridge Street, Muswellbrook NSW**, trading since 2014. We're the local **Authorised Agent for HP, Acer, Lexmark** and a **direct Epson partner** — laptops, printers and workstations. Plus phones, tablets, consoles, and onsite IT for business customers across the Upper Hunter. Owner: **Tim Ray**.

## Mood reference

**Working tradesperson's studio, not a clean room.** Natural light where possible, real workshop bench, real tools, real cables on the bench, real customer device. No "Apple keynote" sterility. No staged perfection.

Reference look: Tim's two existing portraits (phone-bench / HP-laptop-bench) — he's in the workshop, doing the work, mid-task, looking at the device, not the camera (except where stated).

## Shot list — 5 portraits + 1 exterior + 1 group

All portraits **4:5 aspect, vertical**. Exterior is also 4:5 vertical. Group shot is **3:2 landscape** (already exists, can be reshot if better).

### 01 · Tablet repair, heat-mat warm

- **Subject:** Tim seated at the bench, replacing the glass / digitiser on an iPad on a heat mat. Two-handed, careful — "don't crack it twice".
- **Framing:** Slight three-quarter angle from in front of him, bench fills lower third, tools visible.
- **Lighting:** Soft window light from camera-left if possible; otherwise overhead workbench lamp + warm fill. Let the heat-mat orange ground the frame.
- **Wear:** Branded polo (dark navy + white panels).
- **Include:** Heat mat (in use), suction lifter, isopropyl bottle, lint-free cloth, plastic spudgers, nitrile gloves.
- **Avoid:** Glass-cracks-visible-on-the-new-glass (use a clean piece). Posed smiles at the lens. Thick gloves that look bulky.

### 02 · Console mid-teardown

- **Subject:** Games console (PS5 / Xbox / Switch) opened on the bench, top shell off, original cooling fan / heatsink visible, fresh replacement fan beside it.
- **Framing:** Console fills upper two-thirds; replacement part lower-left as a "before / after" beat. Three-quarter angle.
- **Lighting:** Hard rake from upper-left to pick up dust on the original heatsink — yes, we want the dust in the shot. It's the story.
- **Include:** Compressed-air can on the bench edge, thermal paste tube, fresh thermal pads, Tim's hands entering frame.
- **Avoid:** Showing the console's OEM logo straight-on — angle it. We're photographing the work, not the OEM trademark.

### 03 · Technician onsite at a customer business

- **Subject:** Tim at a customer's front counter / reception, plugged into a printer or PC, kit bag on the floor.
- **Framing:** Eye-level mid-shot. Customer's space around the technician — a receptionist or staff member out of focus in the background is ideal.
- **Lighting:** Available light. Mixed fluorescent + window is fine — don't fight the colour cast, it sells "real job".
- **Wear:** Branded polo, lanyard with ID.
- **Include:** Branded polo, kit bag, laptop bag, customer's branded surroundings (their counter, their phone, their signage).
- **Avoid:** Staged smiles to camera. Tools laid out neatly for the photo. We're documenting a real call.

### 04 · Shopfront exterior — golden hour

- **Subject:** Full shopfront elevation, signage clearly legible.
- **Framing:** Shop centred, sky cropped to a thin strip up top, footpath in lower third. Vertical 4:5.
- **Lighting:** Late golden hour, **~30 minutes before sunset**. Warm rake across the brick. Interior lights on so the windows glow.
- **Include:** Signage, A-frame board if you have one, a customer or owner mid-stride entering the shop.
- **Avoid:** Empty street. Harsh midday sun. Cars parked across the frame.

### 05 · Interior wide — service counter

- **Subject:** Tim and Honey behind the service counter, customer device on the bench between them and camera. "Go to a real human" shot.
- **Framing:** 4:5 portrait. Counter cuts the lower third. Both centred, branded polos visible. Shelving / parts bins in soft background.
- **Lighting:** Existing shop lighting + window fill. Don't fight the colour temperature.
- **Mood:** Approachable. Eye contact, hands on the device or counter.
- **Include:** Branded polos, customer device on a soft mat, Authorised Agent decals if framed naturally.
- **Avoid:** Counter clutter. Anyone looking awkwardly at the lens. Old legacy poster art on the back wall.

### 06 · Tim portrait — "the owner" shot

- **Subject:** Tim, in branded polo, against the shopfront or workshop bench. Confident, approachable, no smile-forced.
- **Framing:** 4:5 portrait, head-and-shoulders.
- **Lighting:** Soft natural light. Backdrop slightly defocused.
- **Use:** Reserved for /about page, Google Business profile, social.

### 07 · Honey portrait — "front of house"

- **Subject:** Honey, in branded polo, at the service counter or shopfront.
- **Framing:** 4:5 portrait. Same treatment as Tim's.
- **Use:** /about, Google Business profile.

## Practical notes

- **Shoot raw + JPEG.** Deliver high-res JPEGs at 4:5 (minimum 2000px on the long edge) for the site.
- **Deliver web crops too.** ~1000×1250 JPEG quality 80–85 for direct site use.
- **Naming:** match the filename to the shot — `tim-tablet-bench.jpg`, `console-teardown.jpg`, `tim-onsite.jpg`, `shopfront-golden-hour.jpg`, `service-counter-tim-honey.jpg`, `tim-portrait.jpg`, `honey-portrait.jpg`.
- **What's already shot:** phone-bench portrait + HP-laptop-bench portrait — both delivered as ChatGPT-generated AI images. Photographer-shot replacements welcome but not urgent.

## Don'ts (across all shots)

- ❌ Stock-feeling "Apple keynote" lighting / sterile sets
- ❌ Posed lens-staring smiles
- ❌ Tools laid out in a flat-lay arrangement
- ❌ OEM logos for brands we're **not** authorised for (Apple/Samsung/Dell/Lenovo) prominent — keep angled / shallow-focus
- ❌ Bright colour casts on the polo (we want the brand blue + ink to read correctly)
